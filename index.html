The 2nd ECOWAS Lassa Fever International Conference (ELFIC 2025) marked a significant milestone in the fight against Lassa fever in West Africa, convening policymakers, scientists, health professionals, and development partners to accelerate readiness for vaccines and strengthen regional preparedness.

Highlights

  • Historic commitment: West African Ministers of Health pledged to advance readiness for Lassa fever vaccines, recognizing the disease's threat to regional health security.
  • Regional solidarity: ECOWAS Ministers committed to co-finance vaccine development and strengthen platforms for clinical trials, laboratories, and community engagement.
  • Vaccine development: Progress of the IAVI Lassa vaccine (Phase IIa) highlighted the need for sustained support to reach licensure.
  • Global partnerships: Over 800 participants fostered collaborations across West Africa and beyond to combat Lassa fever.
  • New path for West Africa: Countries united to co-finance and prepare systems for vaccine development and distribution.
  • Climate & risk: Climate change and population growth could put up to 600 million people at risk by 2050â€”underscoring the need for coordinated preparedness.

Objectives

  • Advance R&D and share scientific progress, including vaccine candidates.
  • Strengthen regional coordination and data/resource sharing to enhance health security.
  • Improve outbreak management systems and multisectoral collaboration.
